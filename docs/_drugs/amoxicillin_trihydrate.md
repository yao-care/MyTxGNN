## Why is This Prediction Reasonable?

From general pharmacological knowledge, Amoxicillin Trihydrate is an aminopenicillin antibiotic that inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding irreversibly to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs), preventing peptidoglycan cross-linking and ultimately causing bacterial cell lysis. It is active against a broad spectrum of Gram-positive organisms and select Gram-negative bacteria. Its established clinical uses include respiratory tract infections, urinary tract infections, otitis media, and skin and soft tissue infections.
